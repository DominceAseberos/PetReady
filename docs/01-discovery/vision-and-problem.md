## 1. The Problem

### Why This Exists

Every year, millions of pets are returned to shelters or abandoned because their owners were unprepared. The data is clear:

- **7–15% of adopted dogs** are returned to shelters after failed adoptions
- **Median return time**: just 3 days for behavioral issues
- **Top return reasons**: behavioral problems, lifestyle mismatch, unexpected costs, multi-pet conflicts
- **95.5% increase** in dog intakes in some cities over 5 years (Chicago, 2020–2025)

### Root Cause

People don't fail at pet ownership because they don't care. They fail because:

1. **The decision is emotional** — "that puppy is so cute" overrides rational planning
2. **Information is scattered** — costs, time commitment, behavioral needs are learned *after* adoption
3. **No structured preparation exists** — no tool simulates what pet ownership actually feels like day-to-day
4. **Quizzes are worthless** — a 2-minute quiz cannot measure whether you'll wake up at 6am for feeding

### Who Suffers

- **Pets**: Trauma of rehoming, shelter overcrowding, euthanasia
- **Owners**: Guilt, financial stress, emotional distress
- **Shelters**: Overwhelmed capacity, repeated processing costs
- **Society**: Stray animals, public health concerns

## 2. The Vision

### One-Line Vision

> **PetReady makes you live through pet ownership before you commit — so you know if you're truly ready.**

### What We're Building

A web platform that replaces the broken "quiz → instant answer → forget" model with a **multi-day interactive simulation** that:

1. Assesses your lifestyle, finances, and living situation
2. Puts you through realistic daily pet care tasks over 3–7 days
3. Throws unexpected challenges at you (emergency vet bills, behavioral incidents)
4. Measures your consistency, responsiveness, and coping
5. Generates a personalized readiness score with specific gap analysis
6. Provides an actionable preparation plan before you adopt

### How a Software Developer Thinks About This

Before writing a single line of code, we asked:

| Question | Our Answer |
|----------|-----------|
| What's the single most important user action? | Completing a multi-day simulation |
| What makes this different from everything else? | Time-based realism — you can't fake consistency |
| What's the riskiest assumption? | Users will come back daily for 3–7 days |
| What's the simplest version that tests this? | 3-day simulation with push notifications |
| Who pays for this? | Shelters (reduced returns) + premium reports |

## 3. Success Criteria

| Metric | Target | Timeframe |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Simulation completion rate | >40% | 3 months post-launch |
| Day-over-day retention during simulation | >60% | Ongoing |
| User-reported usefulness | >80% say "helpful" | Post-simulation survey |
| Pet return rate (PetReady users) | <3% vs 7–15% baseline | 12 months |
| Shelter partnership sign-ups | 10 shelters | 6 months |

---

## 4. What This Is NOT

- ❌ A virtual pet game (no Tamagotchi — this is education, not entertainment)
- ❌ A pet matching service (we answer "are you ready?" not "which pet?")
- ❌ A one-time quiz (the whole point is time-based commitment testing)
- ❌ A pet care app for existing owners (we focus on pre-adoption)
- ❌ A shelter directory (though we link to them)
